              After getting Institute of Emminence tag, KIIT University to focus on research, innovation and foreign students

              Minati Singha| TNN | Sep 9, 2019, 15:50 IST
              After getting the coveted Institute of Eminence (IoE) status KIIT University is going to focus more on research and innovation and also for attracting more foreign students to the varsity.

              TimesPoints

              80
              0 Added

              Keep reading, keep earning TimesPoints!!

              Representational Image
              BHUBANESWAR: After getting the coveted Institute of Eminence (IoE) status KIIT University is going to focus more on research and innovation and also for attracting more foreign students to the varsity.

              "Our aim is to make KIIT University a world class hub of education with major focus on quality research and innovation. Besides, KIIT would try to attract more foreign students in the coming days, mainly from Europe and America," said founder of KIIT University, Achyuta Samanta. Currently around 1000 foreign students from 53 countries are pursuing various undergraduate and post-graduate courses in the university.

              KIIT University is among ten private universities across India and the youngest among all that has been selected for the IoE and it received the letter of intent IoE from the ministry of Human Resources Development on September 4. The IoE status will also provide complete autonomy to the institute to frame course curriculum, collaboration with foreign and Indian institutes, recruit faculty members and decide fee structure.


              The KIIT University will set up Central Research Centre at the cost of Rs200 crore to promote multi-disciplinary research. For research at the global issues the university is focusing on subjects like water, environment and earth and for local issues on subjects ranging from healthcare issues like malaria, tuberculosis, renewable energy, material science and innovations in medical technology.

              "Apart from the Central Research Centre there will be a translational research wing that will identify various areas of research and fields that need interventions. Then our researchers of different faculties would work on it and find out the solutions," said registrar Sasmitarani Samanta.


              Speaking on the challenges the university has faced over the years, Samanta said, "Started from a small rented house and Rs 5000 seed capital 20 years back, no one has ever though that KIIT University will reach at this stage one day. We faced many challenges and obstacles but never compromised on quality of education, which was reflected in our ranks in three categories in most of the national and international rankings for educational institutes."


              Currently KIIT University has as many as 26,000 students and 2500 faculty members in different departments.

              Policy paper on aftercare for young adult orphans to be released

              Rujuta Parekh| TNN | Sep 9, 2019, 03.46 PM IST
              The Pune International Centre is set to release the PIC policy paper on 'Aftercare for Young Adult Orphans' in the city on Sunday.

              TimesPoints

              80
              0 Added

              Keep reading, keep earning TimesPoints!!

              Representational Image
              PUNE: The Pune International Centre is set to release the PIC policy paper on 'Aftercare for Young Adult Orphans' in the city on Sunday.

              The paper has been authored by former Divisional Commissioner of Pune, Prabhakar Karandikar (IAS) and Doctoral Research Scholar from Tata Institute of Social Sciences, Aditya Charegaonkar.

              The paper will be released at the hands of the Deputy Chairman of Maharashtra Legislative Council, Mumbai, Neelam Gorhe.


              The release ceremony will be chaired by the former Chief Secretary of Maharastra, Ajit Nimbalkar (IAS). Member of National Human Rights Commission, New Delhi and retired Indian Foreign Services (IFS) officer Dynaneshwar Mulay will be the Guest of Honour. Other Guests of Honor attending the event are Former Justice of the Bombay High Court, Shalini Phansalkar-Joshi, Member secretary of Maharashtra Child Rights Protection Commission, Mumbai, Seema Vyas (IAS), Commissioner of Women and Child Development, Government of Maharashtra, Hrishikesh Yashod (IAS) and President of Udayan Care, New Delhi, Kiran Modi.

              The ceremony is scheduled to be held at 11 am at Yashwantrao Chavan Academy of Development Administration (YASHADA) at Baner Road.


              PIC has, in the past, released PIC policy papers on an assortment of subjects ranging from pathway to taking Pune to Carbon Neutrality by 2030, Innovating India - Public Policy Agenda, Sanitation for all, Make in India - Success Stories - Lessons Learnt and many others.

              IIFT Registration 2019: Application process begins today at iift.nta.nic.in

              TOI-Online | Sep 9, 2019, 03.44 PM IST
              National Testing Agency (NTA) has invited online applications for the registration of Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) MBA (International Business)on its official website.

              TimesPoints

              80
              0 Added

              Keep reading, keep earning TimesPoints!!

              Representative Imgae
              NEW DELHI: National Testing Agency (NTA) has invited online applications for the registration of Indian Institute of Foreign Trade (IIFT) MBA (International Business)on its official website. The interested candidates can apply online for IIFT MBA (IB) 2020 through the official website of NTA — iift.nta.nic.in - from today i.e., September 9, 2019.

              The last date to submit online applications for IIFT MBA 2020 is October 25, 2019. NTA will conduct the IIFT MBA (IB) entrance examination on December 1, 2019. The admit card for the same would be released in November.

              As per the information bulletin, NTA will announce the results of IIFT MBA (IB) 2020 result on December 11, 2019.

              Eligibility:

              • The applicant should have recognized Bachelor's degree of minimum 3 years' duration in any discipline with minimum 50% marks [45% for the candidates belonging to Scheduled Caste (SC), Scheduled Tribe (ST) and Persons with Disability (PwD) categories] \

              • Candidates appearing for a qualifying examination can also apply subject to the submission of proof of requisite qualification by 7th October 2020.


              • There is no age limit for appearing in this examination.

              NTA CSIR NET December 2019: Registration begins at csirnet.nta.nic.in

              TOI-Online | Sep 9, 2019, 12.49 PM IST
              National Testing Agency (NTA) will conduct CSIR - National Eligibility Test(CSIR-NET) December 2019 December 15, 2019.
              NEW DELHI: National Testing Agency (NTA) will conduct CSIR - National Eligibility Test(CSIR-NET) December 2019 December 15, 2019. The interested candidates who would like to appear for CSIR NET December 2019 can apply online from today i.e., September 9, 2019.

              The last date to submit online applications for CSIR UGC NET is October 9, 2019. Candidates can pay the fee up to October 10, 2019. From this year onwards, NTA will conduct the CSIR NET examination. A notification on CSIR website reads “ CSIR-UGC National Eligibility Test (NET) for Junior Research Fellowship (JRF) & Eligibility Test for Lectureship/Assistant Professorship will be conducted through National Testing Agency in Computer Based Test (CBT) Mode.”

              NTA will issue the admit card for CSIR UGC NET December 2019 on November 9, 2019. The CSIR UGC NET 2019 would be conducted on December 15, 2019, and results for the same would be announced by December 31, 2019.

              NTA is conducting CSIR NET for determining the eligibility of Indian Nationals for the award of Junior Research Fellowships (JRF) and for determining eligibility for appointment as Lecturer (LS) in the areas of Chemical Sciences, Earth, Atmospheric, Ocean and Planetary Sciences, Life Sciences, Mathematical Sciences, Physical Sciences. The award of Junior Research Fellowship to the successful eligible candidates will depend on their finding admission/placement in a University/ National laboratory/ Institution of higher learning and research, as applicable.

              The test will be conducted in Computer Based Test (CBT) mode in two shifts as given below.


              Shift Timing
              Shift – 1 (Morning) 09:30 am to 12:30 pm (IST)
              Shift – 2 (Afternoon) 02:30 pm to 05:30 pm (IST)

              Last date to apply 09.10.2019
              Admit card 09.11.2019
              CSIR NET Exam 15.12.2019
              Result 31.12.2019
